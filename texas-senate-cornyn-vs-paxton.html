Strategic Takeaway

The Meta ad data reveals a dramatic asymmetry in how these two candidates are approaching digital advertising for the March 3rd primary. Cornyn is running a well-funded, multi-pronged Meta operation — 59 official ads plus a dedicated 5-ad attack page — with a combined estimated spend of $124K–$154K generating over 7 million impressions. Paxton, by contrast, has spent just $4.5K–$5.6K on 7 modest ads on Meta.

Cornyn's strategy is built around four pillars: (1) tight alignment with President Trump ("99% voting record," Laken Riley Act, border security), (2) endorsement validation from the National Border Patrol Council, Rick Perry, and Texas Federation of Republican Women, (3) aggressive character attacks on Paxton via the "Crooked Ken" page targeting his impeachment, business dealings, and cultural credibility gaps, and (4) a recent election integrity push via the SAVE Act (3 ads launched Jan 29), adding voter ID and election security to his messaging portfolio. His ads are overwhelmingly Texas-targeted, running on both Facebook and Instagram.

Paxton's Meta presence is minimal and almost entirely fundraising-focused. His ads label Cornyn a "Fake Republican" with a "far-left partner" but lack the volume or spend to compete on impressions. This suggests Paxton is either investing heavily in other channels (direct mail, email, earned media, rallies) or running a lower-budget campaign overall. His strength likely comes from grassroots name recognition as AG and alignment with the MAGA base, rather than paid digital reach.

The most revealing detail: Cornyn is spending more on attacking Paxton ($35K–$43K via "Crooked Ken") than Paxton is spending on his entire Meta ad campaign ($4.5K–$5.6K). This indicates Cornyn views Paxton as a serious threat despite the spending gap, and is investing heavily in defining Paxton negatively before Paxton can define himself.

The outside money gap is equally notable. Despite this being one of the most closely-watched Senate primaries in the country, no PACs, Super PACs, or independent expenditure groups are running Meta ads in this race. The "Crooked Ken" attack page — which functions like a Super PAC operation — is actually funded by Cornyn's own campaign committee. The only other non-candidate presence is Texas Scorecard, a conservative media outlet with modest spend (~$1.5K) that leans editorially anti-Cornyn. This means the Meta ad battlefield is being fought almost entirely candidate-to-candidate, with Cornyn dominating through sheer volume and an aggressive attack strategy that Paxton has not matched on this platform.
