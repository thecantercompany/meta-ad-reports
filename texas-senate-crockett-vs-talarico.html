Spending vs. volume paradox: Crockett has run more ads (189 vs. 179) but Talarico has spent ~$500K–$600K more and generated ~55 million impressions vs. Crockett's ~34 million — a 1.6:1 impressions advantage. Strategic Takeaway

The Democratic primary for Texas' U.S. Senate seat is a $4+ million Meta ad war generating nearly 90 million combined impressions — dramatically outscaling the Republican primary on this platform. Talarico has spent $2.4M–$2.8M (55M impressions) while Crockett has spent $1.9M–$2.2M (34M impressions), making this one of the most expensive primary-stage digital races in the country.

But where is the money actually going? Ad-level targeting data reveals that much of this spend is chasing out-of-state donor dollars, not persuading Texas primary voters. Crockett's top ad reaches only 15.6% Texas audience — the rest flows to California, Georgia, New York, Maryland, and 37 other states. Talarico's biggest ad is 32.7% Texas, better but still two-thirds national. Both campaigns are using Meta primarily as a national fundraising platform, not a Texas voter persuasion tool.

The strategies couldn't be more different. Talarico takes a high-roller, video-first approach: fewer ads but massive individual buys. Single ads at $125K, $150K, and even $175K+ are common, with roughly half his ads being video-only — a bet on visual storytelling and scroll-stopping creative. He sustains spending at $150K–$300K per week through the critical Dec–Jan stretch, and his most recent ads promote in-person rallies (Conroe, Feb 5) alongside fundraising. Crockett takes a diversified, text-forward approach: more total ads (189 vs. 179) but with smaller individual spends ($1K–$30K each), running many variants of the same creative to test messaging and audiences across both English and Spanish. She deploys in concentrated bursts — 48 ads in a single week in early December, 33 ads in late January.

Messaging tells a clear story. Crockett is running an issue-driven campaign on Meta — she leads with specific policy positions (opposing ICE funding, impeaching Kristi Noem) and positions herself as "fighting back." She's also the only candidate in either primary running Spanish-language ads, signaling outreach to Latino voters. Talarico's ads are overwhelmingly fundraising-focused, built around urgency ("surge of donations," "next 24 hours," "finish line") and grassroots framing ("billionaire mega-donors vs. the people"). He tells donors he takes "no corporate PAC money" — positioning himself as purely people-funded.

Unlike the GOP primary, there are zero attack ads. Neither Crockett nor Talarico has run a single ad attacking the other on Meta. This is a stark contrast to the Cornyn vs. Paxton race, where Cornyn operates a dedicated "Crooked Ken" attack page. The Democratic primary is being fought entirely on positive positioning and fundraising.

The outside money tells a different story than expected. Lone Star Rising PAC has quietly spent $105K–$120K on Meta running 3 ads explicitly supporting Talarico — generating roughly 3 million impressions that are 99.99% Texas-targeted. This is the only significant PAC spending in either primary on Meta. No Dem Left Behind PAC also names Talarico as a national Senate target alongside Ossoff, Brown, and Cooper. Meanwhile, zero PAC dollars support Crockett on this platform. The irony: the PAC is doing the Texas voter persuasion work that the campaigns themselves are not — and it's flowing entirely in one direction.
