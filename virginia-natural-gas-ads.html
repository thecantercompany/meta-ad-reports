Strategic Takeaway

The Virginia natural gas ad landscape is overwhelmingly shaped by anti-gas, pro-solar messaging. The Chesapeake Climate Action Network is the dominant Virginia-specific spender at $46K–$64K, running a sustained "solar is the solution to rising costs" campaign with single ads exceeding $15K in spend. Dogwood reinforces this with educational content, and Virginians for Clean Energy adds legislative accountability framing — blaming utilities and PJM for over-reliance on gas.

On the pro-gas side, no Virginia-specific organization is running advocacy ads. The pro-gas narrative in Virginia is entirely carried by national campaigns from Energy Citizens (API) at $635K–$986K and Natural Allies at $27K–$43K. While API's spend dwarfs all others, its messaging is generic ("essential to the economy") and not tailored to Virginia issues like data center demand, Dominion rate cases, or the 2026 legislative session.

The critical gap: Virginia-specific pro-gas voices are absent from Meta advertising. CCAN is framing the energy cost debate around solar vs. gas with Virginia-specific data ("79.4% of Virginians think solar will lower costs"), while the pro-gas side has no localized counter-narrative. The Thomas Jefferson Institute provides neutral policy coverage but isn't running advocacy. This asymmetry means the Virginia-specific political narrative around natural gas is being defined almost entirely by its opponents.
