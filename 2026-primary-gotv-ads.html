The Headline

Heading into the March 3 Texas primary and the lead-up to New Mexico's June primary, nonprofit GOTV advertising on Meta is overwhelmingly the story of one operation: Beto O'Rourke's Powered by People, which spent $693K–$1.27M across 4,701 ads — roughly four out of every five GOTV nonprofit dollars on the platform in TX and NM combined. Everyone else is small, late, and narrowly focused. ACLU of Texas waited until 48 hours before the primary to deploy a county-by-county Election Day reminder operation. League of Women Voters of Texas ran six ads, all on a single day. Houston-area 501(c)(3) BakerRipley executed remarkable hyperlocal work — 609 ads naming individual Fort Bend polling places — but at neighborhood scale and budget. In New Mexico, the entire nonprofit GOTV/issue-advocacy ecosystem on Meta combined spent less than $110K, and most of that was legislative-session issue advocacy, not voter mobilization. The lane that the Democratic Party doesn't fill on Meta — and the Texas Democratic Party spent $300–$498 in this window — is being filled by one man's organization and a handful of small civic nonprofits punching above their weight in the final week.

Advertiser Profiles

Twelve organizations carried the bulk of nonprofit GOTV-adjacent activity on Meta during the window. Each one ran a meaningfully different play. The profiles below quote ad text verbatim from the Ad Library; messaging summaries describe what the organization is actually doing on the platform, not what they say they do elsewhere.

Messaging Themes

Across all 12 advertisers, GOTV messaging falls into four distinct buckets. The mix is heavily skewed toward issue advocacy and registration, with classic election-day turnout messaging compressed into the final week before the Texas primary.

Theme Mix by Advertiser
Approximate share of ad volume per theme. Stacked horizontal bars.

1. Voter Registration

  • Voto Latino: "Registering to vote is fast, simple, and one of the most important…"
  • Powered by People: "reach, register, and help turn out the voters who can make the difference"

Concentrated in two operations. Voto Latino frames registration as a response to immigration policy, not as election prep.

2. Turnout / Election-Day

  • ACLU TX: "Election Day is March 3 and polls are open 7 a.m. – 7 p.m."
  • BakerRipley: "You can vote today at George Memorial Library."
  • Powered by People: "Democrats are showing up at more than double the rate we saw at this point in 2022"

The classic GOTV bucket. Compressed into the final 7 days before the TX primary. Hyperlocal at BakerRipley; statewide-momentum at PbP; county-segmented at ACLU.

3. Voter Education / Info

  • LWV Texas: "Texas Primary Elections choose who will be on the November ballot. With political redistricting, most races are actually…"
  • BakerRipley: "Don't know where to vote? Here are all the local polling places."
  • New Chapter NM: "Follow for key dates, handy poll maps, and direct access to candidate information"

Nonpartisan information. Drives to platforms (VOTE411) or organic page follows. Smallest bucket by dollars but the most clearly nonprofit-aligned.

4. Issue Advocacy Tied to the Vote

  • ProgressNow NM: "SB18 supports New Mexico's leadership in renewable energy"
  • AFP-NM: "Vote NO on SB151 and SB235"
  • OLÉ NM: corporate-accountability resolutions, childcare wages
  • TX Organizing Project: "join the fight for health equity"
  • NM Kids CAN: school-board candidate attacks & endorsements
  • Powered by People: voting-rights / "Republicans are doing everything they can to make it harder to vote"

By far the largest theme bucket by dollars. Most "GOTV" nonprofit spend in this window is functionally legislative-session and cycle-prep advocacy, not "vote on Tuesday" turnout.

The pattern Issue advocacy > registration > turnout > voter info — that's the order by dollars. "Pure GOTV" content (vote-on-this-day, here's-your-polling-place) only spikes in the final week before each election. Most of the 8-month window is filled with legislative-priority advocacy, list-building, and voting-rights organizing — useful for building a turnout machine, but not the same thing as turning out the vote.

06

Notable Absences & Party-Committee Context

The user-defined scope of this report is nonprofits, 501(c)(4)s, and unions — but the absences are part of the story.

Party committees: barely present

Texas Democratic Party: $300–$498 across 2 ads in the entire 8-month window. Effectively zero presence on Meta.

Republican Party of Texas: Did not surface in top-50 spender queries for primary, registration, or turnout terms. Meta is not where the state parties are operating.

Implication: when a nonprofit like Powered by People spends $1M+ on Meta GOTV, it is not supplementing a robust party-committee presence — it is substituting for one.

Conservative-aligned nonprofits in TX

No major conservative 501(c)(4) ran a sustained TX GOTV operation in the window — Texas Public Policy Foundation, Texas Scorecard ($1.1K–$1.9K), and Truth & Liberty did not approach the scale of the progressive advertisers profiled above.

In New Mexico, AFP-NM is the conservative analog at $8.3K–$21.2K, but that is roughly half the Animal Protection Voters / OLÉ / ProgressNow combined progressive total.

Unions: largely off-platform

No major TX or NM union surfaced in top-spender queries during the window for GOTV-relevant search terms. Texas AFT, AFL-CIO, AFSCME, NEA-NM, and the NM Federation of Labor were not detectable as top-50 nonprofit GOTV advertisers on Meta.

Possible reasons: union political/issue work happens through endorsed candidate pages, dedicated PACs, or off-Meta channels (mail, field, member email).

National 501(c)(3)s with TX/NM exposure

The Voter Participation Center spent $114K–$181K nationally on registration, but only ~15% of ads reached TX/NM. New Jersey and Virginia 2025 elections absorbed the bulk of effort.

Color the Vote / Poder Latinx ($232K–$305K) targeted Pennsylvania and Georgia registration deadlines, not TX/NM.

Students for Voting Justice (Center for Common Ground) ran ~$25K–$39K but Virginia-only.

Methodology

Source: Meta Ad Library API (political & issue ads), country filter US, delivery date range 2025-07-01 through 2026-03-03. Discovery via state-level get_top_spenders queries scoped to TX and NM with GOTV-related search terms (registration, primary, early voting, vote, voter ID, polling place) plus seed-name lookups for known nonprofit GOTV operations from the 2024 cycle. Per-page totals via get_page_spend_summary; geographic verification via analyze_spend_by_region; ad copy via get_page_ads and search_political_ads.

Spend reported as upper-bound dollar figures from Meta's spend ranges. Ad counts are total political/issue ads from the page in the window, not filtered to GOTV-only — most in-scope advertisers run only a small mix of non-GOTV political content, so totals are reasonable proxies. Verbatim ad excerpts are quoted under the report's copyright rules: maximum ~15 words per quote, attributed to the funded-by entity and delivery date. Longer creative is paraphrased.

Inclusion criteria: 501(c)(3) civic-engagement nonprofits, 501(c)(4) advocacy organizations, labor unions, and other nonprofit/advocacy entities whose Meta activity in the window includes any of: voter registration, turnout/mobilization, voter education, or issue advocacy tied to voting. Excluded from headline analysis: candidate-affiliated PACs, party committees (TDP, RPT, NM Dem/GOP), candidate campaigns, and government election offices (TX SoS, Harris County Elections, Dallas County Elections, etc.). Multi-state operations were retained only when TX or NM was a top delivery state.

Date as of report generation: 2026-05-05. Spend ranges and ad counts will continue to update as Meta backfills late-reported data.
