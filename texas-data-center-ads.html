Strategic Takeaway

The Texas data center ad landscape is dominated by opposition messaging. Of the 12 Texas-specific advertisers, only TXOGA is running pro-development creative — and at just $1.2K–$1.7K in spend, that's vastly outmatched by the combined volume from Clayton Tucker (5 ads), Ben Bius (2 ads), Chris Gober, Rob Altman, Public Citizen, EDF, and Change.org. Three TX-10 congressional candidates are using data centers as a campaign issue. The opposition frame — water, grid strain, rising bills, no real jobs — is cohesive and gaining traction. Meanwhile, the largest spenders reaching Texas (Market News Now, Money & Markets) are running investment-angle ads, not advocacy, meaning the political narrative is largely being shaped by opponents.
