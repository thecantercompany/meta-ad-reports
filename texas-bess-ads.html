Key Insight GoodPower is running a sophisticated, multi-track advocacy operation with Texas as its primary target. The BESS explainer ad alone reached 250K–300K impressions. Their Winter Storm Fern response deployed within days of the storm with 11+ variants — suggesting a well-funded, rapid-response digital operation. This is by far the most active pro-BESS advertiser in the state.
Strategic Takeaway

The BESS ad landscape in Texas is dominated by pro-development messaging from a single advertiser: GoodPower, which is running 110 ads at $75K–$104K in total spend with Texas as its #1 target state. GoodPower's messaging is sophisticated, covering BESS education, property value myth-busting, storm resilience, and economic benefits for schools and local services.

Anti-BESS opposition is hyperlocal and low-budget but politically potent. Dan Smith in Waller County is using a BESS siting fight near Katy High School as a central campaign issue for county commissioner. "Don't BESS With Menard" represents grassroots Menard County opposition. Both follow the familiar safety-near-schools, property values, fire risk playbook.

The Texas Sierra Club and Greenlight Action add legislative accountability and YIMBY framing, respectively, but at much lower spend levels. Notably absent: any industry trade association advertising (no TXOGA, no Texas Solar Power Association, no storage-developer branded pages). The entire pro-BESS digital narrative in Texas is being carried by GoodPower and a handful of national advocacy groups.
